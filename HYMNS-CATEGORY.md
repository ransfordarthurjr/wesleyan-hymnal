The Methodist Hymn Book (MHB), primarily based on the landmark 1933 edition, is structurally categorized into 12 major thematic sections. These categories organize the 984 hymns to guide congregants logically through the nature of God, the process of salvation, the Christian walk, church sacraments, and special calendar events. [1, 2, 3, 4] 
------------------------------
## I. Adoration (Hymns 1 – 89)
Focused entirely on praising the triune God, this section includes introductory calls to worship and expressions of awe. [5, 6] 

* Key themes: Praise to the Redeemer, the Holy Trinity, and the majesty of God.
* Famous example: MHB 1 (O for a thousand tongues to sing). [5, 6, 7, 8, 9] 

## II. Creation and Providence (Hymns 90 – 107)
These hymns celebrate God as the Sustainer and Maker of the universe. [5] 

* Key themes: Nature, divine protection, and daily guidance.
* Famous example: MHB 104 (The spacious firmament on high). [10] 

## III. The Lord Jesus Christ (Hymns 108 – 181)
Chronicles the earthly life, sacrifice, and divinity of Christ. [5, 8] 

* Key sub-sections: His Incarnation (Christmas), Passion (Good Friday), Resurrection (Easter), and Ascension.
* Famous example: MHB 110 (Hark! the herald angels sing). [5, 11] 

## IV. The Holy Spirit (Hymns 182 – 205)
Dedicated to the third person of the Trinity, emphasizing empowerment, comfort, and the birth of the Church. [5, 8] 

* Key themes: Pentecost, illumination, and inner renewal.
* Famous example: MHB 182 (Come, Holy Ghost, our hearts inspire). [5, 12] 

## V. Repentance and Conversion (Hymns 206 – 336)
Designed for evangelical invitation and the personal turning away from sin. [5, 8] 

* Key sub-sections: Warning and Inviting (206–236); Penitence and Trust (237–336).
* Famous example: MHB 321 (Rock of Ages, cleft for me). [5] 

## VI. The Christian Life (Hymns 337 – 632)
The largest block of hymns, outlining the daily walk, struggles, and growth of a believer. [5, 8] 

* Key sub-sections:
* Believers Rejoicing (337+)
   * Believers Praying (379+)
   * Believers Working (418+)
   * Believers Watching (441+)
   * Conflict and Suffering (454+)
   * Full Salvation / Holiness (514+)
   * The Hope of Heaven (601+) [5] 
* Famous example: MHB 371 (And can it be that I should gain). [13] 

## VII. Christian Ordinances and Institutions (Hymns 633 – 705) [14] 
Centered around the foundational operations and physical sacred spaces of the Church. [5] 

* Key sub-sections: The Holy Scriptures, The Lord's Day (Sabbath), The House of God, The Ministry, Baptism, and The Lord's Supper (Holy Communion).
* Famous example: MHB 695/696 (Bread of heaven, on Thee we feed). [5] 

## VIII. The Kingdom of Christ (Hymns 706 – 746)
Focuses heavily on global evangelism, missionary efforts, and Christ's ultimate victory. [5] 

* Key themes: Missions, social renewal, and the extension of God's reign.
* Famous example: MHB 713 (Jesus shall reign where’er the sun). [5, 15] 

## IX. Social and Family Worship (Hymns 747 – 839)
Hymns formatted for intimate gatherings outside of formal Sunday services. [5] 

* Key sub-sections: Fellowship and Prayer, The Family Circle, and Children and Youth.
* Famous example: MHB 808 (Glory to thee, my God, this night). [5, 16] 

## X. Death, Judgment, and the Future State (Hymns 840 – 886)
Deals with mortality, eschatology, the final judgment, and eternal life. [5] 

* Key themes: Remembrance, consolation during grief, and eternity. [5] 

## XI. Special Occasions (Hymns 887 – 930)
Tailored to specific dates on the civic or liturgical calendar. [5] 

* Key sub-sections: Watchnight and New Year, Covenant Service, Thanksgiving, and National Humiliation.
* Famous example: MHB 894 (Come, let us use the grace divine — traditionally sung during the Methodist Covenant Service). [5] 

## XII. Doxologies, Benedictions, and Chants (Hymns 931 – 984)
Short liturgical responsive readings, closing blessings, and musical dismissals used to wrap up formal worship services. [5, 17]
